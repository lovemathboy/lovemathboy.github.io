--- Day 25: Christmas Tree Farm ---

Part 1

Your boss has tasked you with investigating a Christmas tree cavern.

There are several elves still decorating the Christmas trees. However, their main worry is their ability to fit all the presents below the trees.

The presents come in a few standard but very weird shapes, and the shapes need to all fit into pre-defined 2D regions. The presents are also not allowed to overlap or stack.

The AoC Elves have a summary of the situation (your puzzle input) for you. First, it contains a list of the presents' shapes, where the region of '#' denotes the shape of the present. Second, it contains the size of the region under each tree and a list of the number of presents of each shape that need to fit into that region. Lastly, it has the floorplan of the region, where a '.' denotes where a present can be placed and '#' denotes a type of obstruction.

The first section lists the standard present shapes. For convenience, each shape starts with its index and a colon; then, the shape is displayed visually, where '#' is part of the shape and '.' is not.

The second section lists the regions under the trees. Each line starts with the width and length of the region, followed by the layout of the region.

E.g. 5x12 means the region is 12 units wide and 5 units long. The rest of the line describes the presents that need to fit into that region by listing the quantity of each shape of present; 1 0 1 0 3 2 means you need to fit one present with shape index 0, no presents with shape index 1, one present with shape index 2, no presents with shape index 3, three presents with shape index 4, and two presents with shape index 5. The following

Then, the next 5 (e.g.) lines contains 12 characters each, where '.' represents space for presents, and '#' represents an obstruction where presents cannot be placed.

Presents can be rotated and flipped as necessary to make them fit in the available space, but they have to always be placed perfectly on the grid. Shapes can't overlap (that is, the # part from two different presents can't go in the same place on the grid), but they can fit together (that is, the . part in a present's shape's diagram does not block another present from occupying that space on the grid).

So, in this example, regions 1 and 2 can fit all of their listed presents. The final answer is the sum of the region IDs that can fit every presents (the IDs are numbered sequentially starting from 1), which is 1 + 2 = 3.

Consider the regions beneath each tree and the presents the Elves would like to fit into each of them. What is the sum of IDs of every region that can fit all the presents?
