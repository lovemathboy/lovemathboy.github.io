--- Day 14: Flip-Flop ---

Part 1

Your boss has given you a circuit made up of flip-flops. Each flip-flop receives a signal as input and has two possible outputs.

Each signal sent to a flip-flop will cause it to alternate outputs. I.e. when a flip-flop first receives a signal, it will forward the signal to the first output. When the flip-flop receives the next signal, it will forward the signal to the second output, and so on. Each flip-flop is also given a 3-letter code name in lowercase.

You operate an input terminal labeled INP. There are two output terminals: OUT and BIN. Every signal will eventually end up at OUT (and is processed by some magical machine) or BIN (and thrown away). Your boss tasks you with finding out how many signals get sent to OUT after sending 123456 signals out of INP.

It is guaranteed that this circuit does not contain any cycles.

Example Input:

INP: abc
abc: def ghi
def: OUT ghi
ghi: OUT BIN
