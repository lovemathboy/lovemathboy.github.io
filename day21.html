--- Day 21: Grid Traversal v2 ---

Part 1

It's Sunday, which means your boss has assigned you a simple task: connecting outlets on a power grid!

You're given multiple square grids of '#' and '.'s, where '#' represents an obstacle and '.' represents an empty cell.

On each square grid, there are two outlet 'O's, find the minimum number of '.'s you need to convert to 'O's such that every 'O' is one connected region!

There are multiple square grids in the input, the final answer is the product of the number of conversions you need

Example Input:

#####
#O#.#
#...O
#.#.#
#####

###O#
#O#.#
#.#.#
#...#
##.##

The number of '.' to 'O' conversions are 3 and 6 respectively as follows:

#####
#O#.#
#OOOO
#.#.#
#####

###O#
#O#O#
#O#O#
#OOO#
##..#

The final answer is 3 * 6 = 18

What is the product of the number of '.' to 'O' conversions in every grid in your input?
